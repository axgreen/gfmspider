Hello All,

Last Saturday, to the shock of everyone who knows him, Jeff Lucas was struck by a terrible accident. A failed deck railing collapsed, and after falling 15 ft, he landed on his back. A T8-T9 spinal fracture occurred, leaving Jeff with no feeling in or control over anything below his upper abdomen.

We are unsure if a portion of his paralysis is temporary, or if this will be a permanent reality for him, but for now, Jeff will live life as a paraplegic.

Weve had so many people reach out to us, so weve created this GoFundMe for those who want to help.

One of the biggest hurdles Jeff and his family will face moving forward is the struggle of regaining the quality of life they once had. We would be incredibly grateful for any financial support you can offer to make this happen. The expenses are sure to stack up quickly; current and future medical bills, wheelchairs, wheelchair ramps, widening doors, modifying bathrooms/kitchens, wheelchair lifts for stairs, a new car that can accommodate a disabled driver are all priorities, but they only represent a portion of the financial burden his family will be bearing during his recovery.

Please, be generous. Help Jeff and his family maintain the quality of life theyve worked so hard to build. Your support is appreciated more than you can know.

We love you and thank you from the bottom of our hearts. We will leave off with a message that Jeff wants you to hear:

Every single one of you reading this needs to go hug and kiss your family, right now, at least electronically if physically isnt an option. Because you never know when your life will change so suddenly and so violently and so permanently...

Stop fighting over nonsense that doesnt matter. Stop hating on people who think differently than you. Stop working so much. Stop, for a moment, and consider the possibility that you may not be right, and that there may not BE a right.

Instead, go to fun places and do fun things with the ones that you love. Quickly, before you cant.

God Bless you all and thank you from the bottom of our hearts.

Update 4
Posted by Jeffrey Lucas
1 day ago
    Share
 
 
Good morning my Friends,

Day 14. Last night 41 staples were removed from my back, which brings me one step closer to my long anticipated date with an industrial strength back scratcher. Maybe a belt sander...havent decided yet.

Physical therapy is going remarkably well, I am shocked at how fast Im getting the hang of reestablishing my balance, and while seat-to-seat transfers are still a struggle, Im slowly getting the hang of it. Im feeling really positive about my stay at Sunnyview this far, the staff has been the perfect balance between drill instructors and care givers.

At home, the contract has been signed, designs have been finalized, and the home modifications to prepare for my arrival will be complete 6 weeks from today. My only hope is that I can extend me stay here until they are complete, which may be an epic battle against the insurance authorities on multiple fronts.

All-in-all, Im still clinging to my positive attitude despite the constant pain in my sternum and bruised ribs that refuse to relent. But, all the help that you have given - the food, care for my son, play dates, help with home design, and of course your incredibly generous donations - in all its forms, gives me all the strength I need to succeed. Thank you all.

Love Each Other More.

Update 3
Posted by Jeffrey Lucas
5 days ago
    Share
 
 
Hello Everyone,

Here we are on day 10, and my stay at Sunnyview has this far been a pleasant one. We ran into a slight setback on Tuesday, when I re-dislocated my sternum during physical therapy. The good news is that no permanent damage was done - we just need to wait for it to heal. However, my therapy regimen does not allow for the time required for me to lie in bed while it heals, so I will have to power through it. It will hurt, but with the support of my family, and all of you, I am not afraid.

At home, my family continues to design the home modifications that will transform our abode to maximize its wheelchair accessibility. Regardless of which route we take, this will be an arduous and expensive project, but everyones continued support has and will continue to aid us in bringing this to fruition.

Any thank you for the unfathomable support that you have provided my family that I can muster falls flat on my lips; it will never be enough to truly convey my gratitude. I truly love you all.

Love Each Other More.

Update 2
Posted by Jeffrey Lucas
8 days ago
    Share
 
 
Good morning everyone. I just wanted to drop a shotgun blast update to save my index finger from blistering - so many of you have reached out to my family and I with kindness and generosity - you are a fierce army of compassion, hundreds strong. I am shocked on a daily basis about the impact that you say that I have had on you, and your desires to reciprocate are nothing short of staggering.

I have been moved from Albany medical center to Sunnyview rehab hospital in Schenectady. While my pain is still significant, Im finally feeling my strength starting to return. The difference in comfort here is like night and day, but my schedule for learning to cope with my disability through rehabilitation and learning over the next 3-4 weeks will be nothing short of grueling. At home, my family and friends work to regain our quality of life, and have begun the immensely complicated and expensive job of home alteration to prepare for my return.

I love and appreciate you all more than you will ever know. Thank you for the kindness and generosity that you have shown my family, we are forever in your debt.

Love each other more.
